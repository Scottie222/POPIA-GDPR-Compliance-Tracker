# Case Study - WhatsApp POPIA Enforcement 2024

## Background

In 2024, South Africa's Information Regulator found that WhatsApp
was applying stronger data privacy protections to European users
under GDPR than to South African users under POPIA. Both laws
require the same standard. This triggered a formal enforcement action.

## Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2021 | Information Regulator begins engaging WhatsApp |
| April 2024 | Formal compliance assessment launched |
| 10 September 2024 | Enforcement notice issued to WhatsApp |
| 10 November 2024 | WhatsApp deadline to comply |
| April 2025 | Enforcement notice published publicly |

## POPIA Sections Violated

| Section | Requirement | What WhatsApp Did Wrong |
|---------|------------|------------------------|
| Section 8 | Lawfulness of processing | No lawful basis demonstrated |
| Section 9 | Processing limitation | Collected more data than necessary |
| Section 11 | Consent | Consent was forced and not informed |
| Section 13 | Purpose specification | Did not explain why data was collected |
| Section 15 | Further processing | Used data for new purposes without permission |
| Section 17 | Openness | No records of processing activities kept |
| Section 19 | Security safeguards | Weak technical and organisational measures |

## Penalties Issued

| Penalty | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Administrative fine | Up to R10 million |
| Criminal liability | Up to 10 years imprisonment |
| Compliance order | Personal Information Impact Assessment required |
| Deadline | 60 days to submit compliance report |

## What Could Have Prevented This

| Action Required | POPIA Section |
|----------------|--------------|
| Appoint an Information Officer | Section 55 |
| Update privacy notice for SA users | Section 13 |
| Make consent voluntary and specific | Section 11 |
| Conduct a Personal Information Impact Assessment | Section 19 |
| Restrict cross-border data transfers | Section 72 |

## Key Lesson

A company can be GDPR compliant in Europe and still violate POPIA
in South Africa. Local compliance is not automatic. This tracker
is built to detect that exact gap before a regulator does.
